## 3. Key Papers Found — Assessment

### 3.1 Papers on Quantum Walks + Möbius Geometry

**Paper A: Moradi & Annabestani, "Möbius Quantum Walk" (arXiv:1706.04817, J. Phys. A 50, 2017)**
- Defines a "Möbius factor" α as a rotation parameter in an extra Hilbert space added to a Hadamard QWC.
- α = 1/2 gives a Möbius-like structure; studies limiting distribution and mixing time.
- **No topological analysis.** No Floquet spectrum. No phase diagram. No deck operator construction. No chiral symmetry analysis.
- **Pre-emption status:** NOT pre-empting. Different construction (extra rotation space, not anti-periodic BC on coin). Different questions (mixing time, not topology). Must be cited as the only prior "Möbius quantum walk" paper, with a clear explanation of how our construction differs.

**Paper B: Li et al., "Quantum walks on two kinds of two-dimensional models" (Int. J. Theor. Phys. 54, 2015)**
- Numerical study of DTQW on cylindrical strip and Möbius strip as 2D graphs.
- Studies crossing properties, dependence on initial state and system size.
- **No topological analysis.** No spectrum characterization. No Floquet theory.
- **Pre-emption status:** NOT pre-empting. Cite as prior numerical work on DTQW on Möbius strip geometry.

**Paper C: Delanty & Steel, "Discretely-observable continuous time quantum walks on Möbius strips" (PRA 86, 043821, 2012)**
- CTQW on Möbius strips in 3D integrated photonics.
- Studies two-photon correlations, time-dependent symmetries.
- **Continuous-time, not discrete-time.** No topological classification.
- **Pre-emption status:** NOT pre-empting. Different walk type (CTQW). Cite as prior Möbius-geometry quantum walk work.

**Paper D: Dai et al., "Continuous-time quantum walks on nonorientable surfaces: Analytical solutions for Möbius strips and Klein bottles" (2012)**
- Analytical CTQW on 2D lattices with twisted BCs (Möbius strips, Klein bottles).
- Studies eigenvalue spectra, transport efficiency, limiting probabilities.
- Finds asymmetric limiting-probability behavior specific to non-orientable surfaces.
- **Continuous-time, not discrete-time.** No topological invariants. No Floquet analysis.
- **Pre-emption status:** NOT pre-empting. Closest prior work on CTQW spectra on Möbius geometry — must cite. The eigenvalue spectrum discussion is for CTQW Hamiltonians, not Floquet unitaries; the half-odd-integer momentum quantization (T1) is a DTQW/Floquet statement.

### 3.2 Papers on Topology + Möbius Geometry (Non-QW)

**Paper E: Beugeling, Quelle, Morais Smith, "Nontrivial topological states on a Möbius band" (PRB 89, 235112, 2014)**
- Studies topological insulators on a Möbius band (tight-binding Hamiltonian, not QW).
- Key finding: Chern number (QHE invariant) cannot be defined on non-orientable surfaces; quantum spin Hall effect can exist via the orientable double cover.
- **Not a quantum walk paper.** Static Hamiltonian. But the orientability argument about topological invariants is directly relevant to the T2 discussion.
- **Pre-emption status:** NOT pre-empting (different dynamical framework — Hamiltonian, not Floquet DTQW). **Must cite prominently** — their argument about Chern number obstruction on non-orientable surfaces is the closest conceptual predecessor to T2.

**Paper F: Quelle, Beugeling, Morais Smith, "Topological Floquet states on a Möbius band irradiated by circularly polarised light" (Solid State Commun. 215-216, 2014)**
- Extends Paper E to Floquet setting: graphene Möbius band + circularly polarised light.
- Uses Peierls substitution in a tight-binding Hamiltonian (Floquet-Hamiltonian, not DTQW).
- Key finding: Non-orientability prevents homogeneous QHE; uses domain-wall irradiation configuration.
- **Not a DTQW.** Uses Floquet-Hamiltonian framework, not a unitary step operator with coin. No split-step walk, no chiral DTQW classification, no winding numbers.
- **Pre-emption status:** NOT pre-empting. **Must cite prominently** — closest work combining Möbius geometry with Floquet topology, but in the continuous-drive Hamiltonian setting, not the DTQW/unitary-step-operator setting. Our paper should explicitly position itself relative to this: "Quelle et al. studied Floquet topology on a Möbius band in the continuous-drive Hamiltonian setting; we study the complementary discrete-time quantum walk setting, where the topological classification (ℤ × ℤ chiral winding numbers) and the Floquet structure (quasi-energy periodicity, 0 and π edge modes) differ qualitatively."

**Paper G: "Floquet Möbius topological insulators" (arXiv:2506.01401, PRB 2025)**
- Uses "Möbius" in the **band-structure sense**: Möbius-twisted edge bands in momentum space, protected by projective translational symmetries.
- **NOT about physical Möbius geometry.** Different meaning of "Möbius."
- **Pre-emption status:** NOT pre-empting. Different concept entirely. Cite only if needed to disambiguate terminology.

### 3.3 Papers on QW Topology + Boundary Conditions

**Paper H: "Boundary-induced coherence in the staggered quantum walk on different topologies" (arXiv:1804.04331, 2018)**
- Studies staggered (coinless) QW on torus, Klein bottle, real projective plane, sphere.
- Klein bottle and real projective plane are non-orientable.
- Studies coherence and entropy, not topological invariants or phase diagrams.
- **Pre-emption status:** NOT pre-empting. Different walk model (staggered/coinless), different observables (coherence, not topology). But establishes that non-orientable BCs have been considered for quantum walks — cite.

**Paper I: Higuchi et al., "Comfortability of quantum walks on embedded graphs on surfaces" (arXiv:2501.06765, 2025)**
- Defines quantum walks on graph embeddings including non-orientable surfaces via twist parameter τ.
- Studies "comfortability" (a walk-specific observable), not topological classification.
- **Pre-emption status:** NOT pre-empting. Different model and observable. Cite as recent work connecting QW to surface topology.

## 5. Gap Analysis

### What has been done (cite specifically):
1. **CTQW on Möbius geometry:** Eigenvalue spectra and transport properties studied analytically (Dai et al. 2012; Delanty & Steel 2012). No topological classification.
2. **DTQW on Möbius with a "rotation" construction:** Moradi & Annabestani 2017. Different model (extra rotation space, not anti-periodic BC). Mixing time only.
3. **Tight-binding Hamiltonian topology on Möbius band:** Beugeling, Quelle, Morais Smith (PRB 2014; SSC 2014). Chern number obstruction, QSHE possible. Floquet extension via continuous driving. Not DTQW.
4. **DTQW topological classification on cylinders/lines:** Kitagawa et al. 2010, Asbóth & Edge 2015, Asbóth & Obuse 2013, Cedzich et al. 2018. Complete for orientable geometries. No non-orientable extension.
5. **QW on non-orientable surfaces (non-topological):** Li et al. 2015 (DTQW, numerical), boundary-coherence paper 2018 (staggered QW), Higuchi et al. 2025 (comfortability).

### What has been claimed but not rigorously shown:
- No one has claimed or shown that anti-periodic BCs on a DTQW produce half-odd-integer momentum quantization with topological consequences. The CTQW literature (Dai et al.) discusses eigenvalue spectra on Möbius lattices but does not connect to Floquet winding numbers or chiral topological classification.

### What is entirely open and feasible:
- **T1:** Half-odd-integer momentum quantization for coined DTQW on Möbius graph — **OPEN.** Straightforward Bloch-Floquet exercise.
- **T2:** Phase-diagram modification / topological invariant reduction — **OPEN.** No prior work on winding numbers evaluated on half-odd-integer momentum lattice.
- **T3:** Bulk-edge correspondence on cut Möbius walker — **OPEN.**
- **T4:** Universality across coined protocols — **OPEN.**
- **T5:** Szegedy contrast — **OPEN.** No prior work on Szegedy walks on non-orientable graphs.

## 7. Risks Identified

1. **Reviewer pushback P2 is partially activated:** Anti-periodic BCs on lattice systems are well-studied in condensed matter (fermion BCs, Bloch theory). The paper must frame the contribution as the *DTQW-specific topological analysis* (Floquet winding numbers, chiral classification, 0/π edge modes) on the non-orientable graph, not merely the spectrum shift. The Beugeling/Quelle/Morais Smith line of work on Hamiltonian systems is the main comparison point.

2. **Terminological collision:** "Floquet Möbius topological insulator" (arXiv:2506.01401) uses "Möbius" for band-structure twists, not physical Möbius geometry. The paper should use "Möbius graph" or "non-orientable Möbius geometry" consistently to avoid confusion.

3. **The Moradi & Annabestani (2017) paper** uses the exact title "Möbius Quantum Walk." Our paper's title must be sufficiently distinct. Suggested: keep the working title "Möbius Quantum Walk: Topological Signatures of Non-Orientable Floquet Geometry" — the subtitle distinguishes it clearly.
