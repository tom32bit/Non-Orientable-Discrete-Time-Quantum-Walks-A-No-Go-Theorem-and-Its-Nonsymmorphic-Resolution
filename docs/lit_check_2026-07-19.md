## New finding (mandatory Part I citation, and an asset)

**Discrete-Time Quantum-Walk & Floquet Topological Insulators via
Distance-Selective Rydberg-Interaction** (Quantum 6, 664, 2022;
arXiv:2101.11412). Popular summary states the platform's long-range
interactions allow "periodic boundary conditions resulting in DTQW over
topological surfaces like torus and Mobius stripe." **No topological analysis
of the Möbius case is performed.** Not pre-empting; it supplies the
experimental-platform hook: a published proposal that can engineer exactly the
boundary conditions and (plausibly) the sublattice-dependent coins our
construction needs. Cite in the introduction and the realizability discussion.

## Standing pre-emption map (unchanged from 2026-05 analyses, re-confirmed)

- Shiozaki–Sato–Gomi 2015 (PRB 91, 155120): static MTI, ℤ₂ by unitary
  nonsymmorphic symmetry. Foundation; not a walk.
- Mochizuki–Bessho–Sato–Obuse 2020 (PRB 102, 035418): DTQW + **time**-glide.
  Closest prior art; different symmetry axis (partial *time* translation);
  2D example; no Möbius-bundle/MTI framing.
- Zhou–Zhang–Pan 2025 (arXiv:2506.01401): Floquet MTI by Hamiltonian quench;
  0 occurrences of DTQW/coin/split-step (verified 2026-05-16).
- Cedzich et al. 2018 (Quantum 2, 95): complete AZ-class index theory for 1D
  symmetric walks; no nonsymmorphic/glide classes.
- Chen et al. 2020/2022: projective translational symmetry, Brillouin Klein
  bottle — static/classical platforms.
- Part I prior art (Moradi–Annabestani 2017; Dai 2012; Delanty–Steel 2012;
  Li 2015; Beugeling/Quelle 2014; Higuchi 2025): unchanged, all re-confirmed
  non-pre-empting in the 2026-05-07 check.
